Collections Access and Transformative Stewardship

Welcome to our Digital Collections “digital conservation lab”:

This is a laboratory space for working with digital collections materials. You can think of it as a “sandbox” or a “workshop,” but we think of it as an extension of the conservation lab. The works below are part of the Cooper Hewitt digital collection. You can learn more about the digital collection here
Like a physical conservation lab, this means things you find here might be messy or incomplete. In the “digital conservation lab” you won't find the “official” or authorized versions of the works, however, what you see here are instead works-in-process. This is because Digital collections like these can be displayed only intermittently in the museum’s in person galleries, but in order to care for them, we need to be able to turn them on and test them out in the meantime.
Here, we can test and troubleshoot different versions of these digital works to understand how they work and how to improve the accessibility of the user experience. This “open lab” is part of a larger project called Collections Access and Transformative Stewardship (CATS).

Our eventual goal is for CATS to address the museum’s growing need for an accessible, digital subsite to investigate preserving and presenting interactive, born-digital works, with the goal of enabling the long-term stewardship of these works. CATS has two intertwined goals 1) Thought leadership to socialize and redefine terms related to access to collections (especially digital collections) and 2) an interactive digital platform that sets a new higher standard for accessibility.
This is the initial demo site to experience works in the collection currently without accessibility features, so that expert user testers can give us input. This sandbox allows testing of collection assets and experimentation with design features while we work different versions. Thank you for visiting.
